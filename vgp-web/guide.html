User Guide

It's finally here. We can now explore the beautiful world of geometry using only voice! But how do we do that? Lets see below. Let's get started by setting our whiteboard title:

set title shapes

White Board Properties

Since we're still in the beginning let's also define the drawing properties that we are going to use:

set line color blue

set fill color white

set size 3

set opacity 0

Basic Shapes Commands

Now that we are 'prepared', lets draw! We'll start with something simple, a point:

draw point A 10 and 12

... and continue with something twice more complex:

draw line MN 5 and 7 and 20 and 30

Easy peasy! Let's draw more complex shapes!

Triangular Shapes

triangle ABC 10 and 40 and 12 and 35 and 15 and 45

rectangular triangle DEF 20 and 35 and 10 and 7

equilateral triangle GHI 30 and 35 and 10

isosceles triangle JKL 4 and 45 and 10 and 150

Quadrilateral Shapes

quadrilateral ABCD 10 and 35 and 20 and 35 and 20 and 45 and 10 and 40

rectangle EFGH 25 and 35 and 8 and 10

parallelogram IJKL 40 and 45 and 5 and 10 and 75

square MNOP 3 and 35 and 5

Circular Shapes

circle C 20 and 30 and 10

By trying all the commands above we ended up with a full board. How do we clear it? Simple:

clear board

Other Commands

Oups! We forgot to save a copy of the drawing or share it? What do we do?

1. Let's undo:

undo

2. Download:

download

3. Share on dropbox:

share on dropbox

Nice. We successfully downloaded and shared our app. What next? ... Ah! We were trying to start a new whiteboard. Use the simple redo command to get one step forward:
redo
Now we got a clean whiteboard again. Let's draw again!

draw square ABCD 5 and 5 and 10

draw equilateral triangle ABC 10 and 10 and 6
Hmmm, do you like this triangle? Let's take a better look at its properties by using:
show properties of ABC

It is too pointy. We'll achieve this using delete ABC Are we stuck now? How do we close the pop-up?

close popup

... Actually wait! You should have learned all the things you need in order to continue drawing by yourself. If you like what we did so far you can keep drawing or start a new whiteboard. Good luck!
